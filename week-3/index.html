Data Conversion Tips & Tricks
A common question about your datasets: unique IDs
Unique IDs should be perpetually tied to the features they identify
The ID scheme should be easy to maintain internally
Another common question about your datasets: trail use fields
Trail use information is important for your end users: visitors and trailgoers
You may need to combine datasets to get trail use information
This is an advantage to managing OpenTrails separately from your internal processes
But we'll get into that in a bit
Using OpenTrails Data
Put it on a map!
One awesome way: OuterSpatial
Getting your data on OuterSpatial is easy as pie!
1. Go to OuterSpatial.com
2. Click on Welcome OpenTrails Students
3. Sign up for OuterSpatial
4. Upload your zipped OpenTrails data
5. See your data on an interactive map!
This map is embeddable
OuterSpatial defaults to open. Magical open data!
That same data can be used in mobile applications like To the Trails and OpenTrails Mobile
If you're interested in adding your data to an existing application or making a mobile trails application specific to your region, contact Trailhead Labs.

Sustainability Plans
Who is going to manage the data? How will it be collected and updated?
Right now, publishing OpenTrails is a one-way process
Data only flows from your internal processes to the dataset where it lives online
In addition to good metadata, how can you make your OpenTrails data useful?
Keep it up to date!
Keep improving it!
Replace null values for recommended features
Update and add trail names
Create trailhead features
Associate trailheads with trail segments
Document new trail segments and add them to the dataset
Correct errors
Reminder: data should flow from your internal processes to OpenTrails
How to manage this? Clear process.
A sustainability plan outlines everyone who touches the OpenTrails data and their roles in publishing and maintaining
Upstream: GIS analysts, park managers
Downstream: visitor-facing staff, marketing, communications, trailgoers
Sustainability plans will vary from agency to agency
To recap, a sustainability plan includes:
  • Making updates upstream
  • Converting data
  • How you're going to use the data
  • Handling downstream feedback
  • Community of practitioners
